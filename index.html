The Origin Story

It wasn't a dream.
It was a way to get through.

I didn’t start my business because I dreamed of being an entrepreneur. I started it at 18 as a way to get through school. Self-employment felt temporary—practical, not purposeful.

And honestly, there was a season when I didn’t even like it. The freedom everyone talks about didn’t feel free. It felt messy, inconsistent, and heavier than it should have been.

That tension forced a turning point.

The Realization

I wasn’t overwhelmed because I was incapable. I was overwhelmed because everything depended on me. I realized the problem wasn’t business—it was the lack of systems.

I built clear, repeatable frameworks for sales, follow-up, leadership, and time management—not to grow faster, but to survive better. What happened next surprised me. Growth accelerated. Confidence increased. And for the first time, being self-employed actually felt sustainable.

Those same systems helped me lead the fastest-growing team in my organization, set multiple growth records, and earn national recognition. All of that was humbling—but it was a byproduct.
